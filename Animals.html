Animals of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, known for its rich biodiversity, is home to a diverse array of animals that inhabit its lush landscapes. The island's tropical climate and varied ecosystems provide habitats for a wide range of species. Sri Lanka boasts a variety of mammals, including the elusive leopard, elephants, and the purple-faced langur. The island's numerous national parks and wildlife reserves, such as Yala and Wilpattu, offer opportunities for wildlife enthusiasts to witness these creatures in their natural habitats. Additionally, Sri Lanka is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 400 species of birds, including colorful kingfishers, vibrant parrots, and migratory birds that make their way to the island. The surrounding oceans are teeming with marine life, with dolphins and whales often spotted off the coast. Overall, the animals of Sri Lanka contribute to the country's unique and captivating natural heritage.
